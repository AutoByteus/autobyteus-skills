# Logic QA checklist (quick but strict)

Use before finalizing `article.md` and before generating slides.

## Scope + thesis
- Thesis is one sentence and matches what the body actually proves.
- The article answers the user’s question/use case (not a different one).
- Claims are appropriately scoped (no overreach).

## Argument flow
- Each section has a claim (not just a topic).
- Each claim has evidence (quote/data/observation) and reasoning (why it follows).
- Transitions explain *why* the next move follows.
- No missing premise: later conclusions don’t rely on undefined terms or unstated facts.

## Counterbalance
- Alternative interpretations or objections are acknowledged (at least the strongest one).
- You clearly separate: observation vs interpretation vs application.

## Clarity
- Jargon is defined once; terms are used consistently.
- Paragraphs are short; signposting words are used (“因为/所以/然而/因此”).

## Integrity
- If you used web research: sources are listed and match the claims made.
- For Bible: quotes match the chosen translation; you don’t import assumptions from outside the passage without labeling them.
- Every load-bearing claim has an evidence anchor (quote/data) or is explicitly labeled as a hypothesis.
- Uncertainty is not hidden: the article either resolves it, narrows claims, or adds a caveat.
- Load-bearing claims are traceable to source IDs in the claim ledger.
- Article-level conclusions do not rest on a single weak or tertiary source.
